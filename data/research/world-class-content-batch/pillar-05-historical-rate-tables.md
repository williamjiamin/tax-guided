## Entry: `uk-corporation-tax-structure-history`

- Proposed route: `/historical/uk/corporation-tax`
- Content type: historical rate table

### Table

| Period | Structure |
|---|---|
| Up to March 31, 2023 | Single 19% corporation tax rate for non-ring-fence profits |
| From April 1, 2023 | Main rate 25%; small profits rate 19%; marginal relief for profits between GBP 50,000 and GBP 250,000 |
| 2025-2026 guidance update | HMRC continues the post-April 2023 split-rate structure in current published guidance |
| 2026-2027 guidance update | HMRC updated the published rates and allowances document on April 1, 2026 |

### Why it matters

This page is historically important because a large amount of internet content still describes the UK as if it still has a simple flat 19% corporation tax rate. The official HMRC rates page makes clear that this ceased to be the whole story from April 1, 2023.

### Sources

- [HMRC rates and allowances for Corporation Tax](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/rates-and-allowances-corporation-tax)

## Entry: `california-first-year-llc-tax-exemption-window`

- Proposed route: `/historical/us/california-llc-first-year-exemption`
- Content type: historical rule-change page

### Table

| Period | Rule |
|---|---|
| Tax years beginning before January 1, 2021 | First-year LLC annual tax generally applied under the normal rule |
| Tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2021 and before January 1, 2024 | California LLCs organizing, registering, or filing to do business were not subject to the $800 annual tax for the first tax year |
| Tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2024 | The temporary first-year relief window ended; normal annual-tax rules resumed |

### Why it matters

This should become a specific historical page because stale articles about California often still treat the temporary relief window as though it were still active. In 2026, that is wrong.

### Sources

- [California LLC page](https://www.ftb.ca.gov/file/business/types/limited-liability-company/index.html)

## Entry: `estonia-distributed-profit-change-2025`

- Proposed route: `/historical/ee/distributed-profit-tax`
- Content type: historical rule-change page

### Table

| Period | Rule |
|---|---|
| Through December 31, 2024 | Estonia still had the lower 14/86 regime for regularly distributed dividends and related 7% withholding in certain cases |
| From January 1, 2025 | EMTA says dividends are taxed only at the company level at 22/78, and the lower 14/86 regime plus the 7% withholding route no longer apply |

### Why it matters

This is one of the most useful non-U.S. historical-rule pages because a lot of founder content about Estonia is outdated. The 2025 change means older summaries can be materially wrong even when the broader "tax on distribution" story still sounds familiar.

### Sources

- [Estonian Tax and Customs Board: Taxation of dividends](https://www.emta.ee/en/business-client/taxes-and-payment/income-and-social-taxes/taxation-dividends)
