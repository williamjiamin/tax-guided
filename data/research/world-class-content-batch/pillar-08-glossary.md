## Suggested seed terms

| Term | Working definition | Why it matters | Core source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Beneficial owner | The person who actually enjoys the benefit of the income rather than merely holding legal title or acting as a nominee. | Treaty and withholding claims often fail when the recipient is not the beneficial owner under the relevant rule. | [HS304](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/non-residents-relief-under-double-taxation-agreements-hs304-self-assessment-helpsheet/hs304-non-residents-relief-under-double-taxation-agreements-2025) |
| Certificate of overseas residence | The tax-residence certificate many non-UK claimants use to support treaty relief claims on UK income. | It is often the first document missing when a non-resident tries to claim UK treaty relief. | [HS304](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/non-residents-relief-under-double-taxation-agreements-hs304-self-assessment-helpsheet/hs304-non-residents-relief-under-double-taxation-agreements-2025) |
| Disregarded entity | A business entity that the IRS does not treat as separate from its owner for federal income-tax purposes. | This is the key default classification concept behind many foreign-owned single-member LLC questions. | [IRS single-member LLC page](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/single-member-limited-liability-companies) |
| Effectively connected income (ECI) | Income effectively connected with the conduct of a trade or business in the United States. | ECI drives whether foreign persons face net-basis U.S. taxation rather than simple gross withholding. | [IRS ECI page](https://www.irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/effectively-connected-income-eci) |
| Foreign-owned U.S. disregarded entity | A U.S. disregarded entity owned directly or indirectly by a foreign person and subject to special information-reporting rules. | This is the audience segment most exposed to Form 5472 failures. | [Instructions for Form 5472](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5472) |
| Gross income | All income from whatever source derived unless another rule excludes it. | This is the starting point for nearly every federal income-tax computation. | [26 U.S.C. Section 61](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=%28title%3A26+section%3A61%28a%29+edition%3Aprelim%29) |
| Marketplace facilitator | A platform operator treated by law as the seller or retailer for certain sales-tax purposes on marketplace sales. | This concept shapes modern e-commerce sales-tax collection responsibility. | [California marketplace guide](https://www.cdtfa.ca.gov/industry/MPFAct.htm) |
| No tax due threshold | The revenue threshold below which a Texas entity may avoid franchise-tax liability, while still potentially owing information-report obligations. | Texas content becomes inaccurate fast if this threshold is ignored or outdated. | [Texas franchise tax page](https://comptroller.texas.gov/taxes/franchise/) |
| Ordinary and necessary expense | A business expense allowed under Section 162 if it meets the statutory standard and related judicial tests. | Founders use this phrase constantly, often without understanding that "ordinary" is a separate legal requirement. | [26 U.S.C. Section 162](https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?edition=2010&num=0&req=granuleid%3AUSC-2010-title26-section162) |
| Permanent establishment (PE) | A treaty concept that can allow a source country to tax business profits when the foreign enterprise has a sufficient taxable presence there. | PE analysis sits at the center of many treaty-based founder and remote-work questions. | [OECD Model Tax Convention](https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2015/10/model-tax-convention-on-income-and-on-capital-2014-full-version_g1g57dda/9789264239081-en.pdf) |
| Pro forma Form 1120 | A mostly blank corporate return shell used by certain foreign-owned disregarded entities solely to attach Form 5472. | Many users do not realize the Form 1120 in this context is not a full corporate-return election. | [Instructions for Form 5472](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5472) |
| Reportable transaction | For Form 5472 purposes, a transaction with a related party that must be reported under the governing rules. | Users often think only revenue counts, when owner funding and other transfers may also matter. | [Instructions for Form 5472](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i5472) |
| Self Assessment | HMRC's system that places responsibility on the taxpayer to file the information needed to calculate the tax charge. | UK individual-filing content is confusing unless this term is anchored in the actual HMRC process. | [HMRC SALF202](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/self-assessment-legal-framework/salf202) |
| Tax treaty | A bilateral agreement intended to address double taxation and allocate taxing rights between countries. | Treaty content should start from the legal agreement, not from social-media summaries. | [IRS Publication 901](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p901) |
| Turnover | The revenue measure used in many non-U.S. tax systems to decide whether a person must register or enters a tax regime. | Ireland VAT thresholds and UAE natural-person corporate-tax thresholds both turn on this kind of measure. | [Ireland VAT thresholds](https://www.revenue.ie/en/vat/vat-registration/who-should-register-for-vat/vat-thresholds.aspx) |
| VAT | Value-added tax, generally charged on taxable supplies of goods and services in systems that use VAT. | VAT rules are central to Switzerland, Ireland, the EU, and many non-U.S. expansion paths. | [Swiss VAT rates](https://www.estv.admin.ch/en/vat-rates-switzerland) |
| Withholding tax | Tax collected at source by the payer on certain payments. | Many cross-border problems are really documentation and withholding problems before they become return-filing problems. | [Publication 515](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p515) |
| Withholding agent | The payer or other responsible party that must collect and remit withholding when the rules require it. | Users often focus on the recipient and forget the payer has the operational burden. | [Withholding exemption on ECI](https://www.irs.gov/individuals/international-taxpayers/withholding-exemption-on-effectively-connected-income) |
